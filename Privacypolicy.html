1. What Payback does

Payback helps you record and organize:

  • shared expense splits,
  • bill payment records and reminders, and
  • income records (for tracking only).

Payback is not a bank, payment service, wallet, accounting service, or debt collection service. We do not process payments, hold money, or guarantee that anyone will pay you.

2. Information we collect

Payback is designed to work without collecting sensitive personal details. We do not ask for your real name or phone number.

We may collect the following categories of information:

A) Account & authentication information

Payback uses third-party sign-in providers (such as Google, Facebook, or Apple ID) to authenticate users.

  • We do not receive or store your third-party password.
  • We store limited identifiers needed to keep you signed in (for example, a provider user ID or a hashed identifier), and basic account metadata required to operate the Service.

Your use of Google/Facebook/Apple ID sign-in is also subject to their terms and privacy policies.

B) User content you choose to enter

You may choose to enter information into the app, including:

  • nicknames,
  • notes/descriptions,
  • expense amounts and categories,
  • bill reminders,
  • income logs (tracking only).

C) Payment links / QR codes (PayMe / Alipay)

To help with settling expenses between friends, Payback may allow you to:

  • paste or enter a PayMe link (PayLink), and/or
  • upload a PayMe or Alipay QR code.

D) Technical and usage data

To operate and secure the Service, we may process limited technical information such as:

  • device/app information (e.g., app version),
  • basic logs related to syncing, reliability, and abuse prevention.

3. How we use information

We use information to:

  • provide the Service features (records, splitting, reminders, syncing),
  • authenticate you and maintain your session,
  • show your payment link/QR code in the limited sharing context you choose (see below),
  • maintain security, prevent abuse, and troubleshoot problems,
  • improve reliability and performance.

4. How sharing works inside Payback (who can see what)

Payback is built around mutual connections and limited sharing.

  • Mutual acceptance: A friend connection requires acceptance. Someone cannot add themselves to your friend list without your approval.
  • Limited visibility of payment methods: Your PayMe link / QR code is visible only to the “splitter” in an expense split where you are involved, so that the splitter can pay you more easily.
  • If there is no expense split between you and another user, they cannot see your payment method.
  • If a user is only the payer in a split, they cannot see other users’ payment methods unless they are the splitter for that split.

5. Sharing with third parties

We do not sell your data.

We do not make your private records public. We may share limited information with:

  • Service providers that help us operate the app (e.g., hosting, syncing, analytics/logging for reliability and abuse prevention), under appropriate protections.
  • Sign-in providers (Google/Facebook/Apple) as part of authentication flows (subject to their policies).

We may also disclose information if required by law or to protect rights, safety, and security.

6. External payments and links (important)

Payback does not verify payment links/QR codes and is not responsible for payments made outside the app. When you tap a PayMe link, it is intended to route you to the PayMe app (if available).

7. Data retention

We keep information for as long as needed to provide the Service and operate it safely (for example, to maintain your records, support syncing, and prevent abuse), unless you request deletion (see “Your choices” below) or we are required/allowed to retain it by law.

8. Your choices & controls

You can:

  • edit or remove some information within the app (where features allow),
  • disconnect friends (where features allow),
  • request deletion of your account and associated data (see Data Deletion section below).

9. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect information. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Children

The Service is not intended for children. If you believe a child has provided personal data, contact us.

11. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the updated version with a new effective date.
